Research

Robotic Guide Dog

A focused collection of projects on robotic mobility assistance for visually impaired users, from quadruped guiding in real environments to collaborative interaction with doors and other environmental affordances.

Ongoing Research

Longitudinal in the wild deployment.

We are currently conducting a longitudinal study to understand how visually impaired users interact with the robotic guide dog over repeated use in everyday settings.

This section will evolve into a living record of the deployment, including field observations, representative demos, and qualitative takeaways from extended use.

Research objectives

  • O1. Identify unmet needs and coverage gaps in everyday BVI navigation.
  • O2. Examine how the robotic guide dog expands support across real-world settings.
  • O3. Derive design implications from prolonged in-situ deployment.
